Project Overview

The CONNECT project transformed small and mid-sized cable TV operators into key players in bridging the digital divide, particularly in underserved and remote communities of the Philippines. By establishing open, software-defined Internet Exchange Points (IXPs) and deploying community-based wireless and fiber-optic networks, the project empowered local ISPs and residents to deliver reliable, low-cost Internet services.
Technical innovations were matched by deep community engagement, civic partnerships, and government collaboration, leading to sustainable Internet connectivity in geographically isolated areas. As a result, thousands of previously unconnected citizens gained access to high-speed Internet, while cable operators enhanced operational capacity and peering performance.

Key Activities

IXP Deployment

Established SCIMIX and CONNECT-IX software-defined Internet Exchange Points to support both research institutions and cable TV operators with advanced peering capabilities.

Network Infrastructure

Installed 26 km of fiber backbone and 8 km of drop cable interconnecting 4 barangays, supported by community-led installation efforts.

Training Programs

Delivered comprehensive BGP and SDN training to cable operator engineers and community technical contacts to build local capacity.

Public WiFi Rollout

Deployed 35 access points providing free Internet access to over 8,900 devices across multiple communities.

Healthcare & Education

Established telemedicine and e-learning facilities through partnerships with civic organizations like Rotary Club.

Knowledge Sharing

Published research findings, participated in PHNOG and USAID BEACON events, and received best paper awards for dissemination.
